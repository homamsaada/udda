## Quick Comparison

| | Zakat al-Mal | Zakat al-Fitr |
|--|-------------|--------------|
| **Ruling** | Obligatory on those who own the Nisab | Obligatory on every Muslim |
| **Condition** | Reaching Nisab + one year passing | Ability to give it |
| **Amount** | 2.5% of wealth | One Sa' of food (≈3 kg) or equivalent |
| **Timing** | Any time the year completes | Before Eid al-Fitr prayer |
| **Who pays?** | Only those at/above Nisab | Every Muslim, including children |
| **Paid for** | Yourself | Yourself and your dependents |

## Zakat al-Mal in Brief

Zakat al-Mal is due on wealth that reaches the Nisab (equivalent to 85g of gold or 595g of silver) and has been held for one lunar year. The rate is 2.5% of total Zakatable wealth.

For a complete guide, see our [comprehensive Zakat calculation guide](/en/blog/how-to-calculate-zakat.html).

## Zakat al-Fitr in Detail

### Who Must Pay?

**Every Muslim** — rich or poor — who possesses more than their food needs for the day of Eid. The head of household pays for all dependents including infants.

### How Much?

One Sa' of the staple food of the region (≈3 kg). Many contemporary scholars permit paying the cash equivalent.

### When?

- **Best time**: On Eid day before the Eid prayer
- **Advance payment**: From the beginning of Ramadan
- **Cannot be delayed** past the Eid prayer without excuse

## Can One Replace the Other?

**No.** They are two independent obligations. You may owe both in the same month.

## Common Mistakes

- **Thinking Zakat al-Fitr replaces Zakat al-Mal**: They are separate obligations.
- **Forgetting to pay for children**: Due for every dependent, including infants.
- **Paying after the Eid prayer**: This invalidates it as Zakat al-Fitr.
- **Confusing recipients**: To learn who among your relatives can receive Zakat, see [Can You Give Zakat to Relatives?](/en/blog/zakat-to-relatives.html)

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can Zakat al-Fitr be paid in cash?**
The Hanafi school and many contemporary scholars permit it.

**Is it due for an unborn child?**
Not obligatory, but recommended by some scholars.

**I forgot to pay — what should I do?**
Pay it immediately even after Eid.
