"For the male, what is equal to the share of two females" — this Quranic phrase is widely known, but many assume it applies universally. In reality, there are cases where women inherit equally or even more than men.

## Sons with Daughters: Males Get Double

When both sons and daughters survive, they share the remainder — **males receive twice the female's share**.

## One Daughter with No Brothers

One daughter alone inherits **one-half** by fixed share.

**Example**: Daughter + full brother → Daughter: 1/2, Brother: 1/2 → **They're equal!**

## Two+ Daughters with No Brothers

They share **two-thirds**.

## Cases Where Women Inherit MORE Than Men

- **Daughter + father**: Daughter gets 1/2, father gets 1/6 + remainder. Daughter's share may be larger.
- **Daughter + uncle**: Daughter gets 1/2, uncle gets remainder — may be less than half.
- **Mother + father + two daughters**: Daughters get 2/3, each parent 1/6 — each daughter gets more than the father.

## How Children Affect Other Heirs

| Heir | Without Children | With Children |
|------|-----------------|--------------|
| Husband | 1/2 | 1/4 |
| Wife | 1/4 | 1/8 |
| Mother | 1/3 | 1/6 |
| Siblings | Inherit | Blocked |

For blocking details see [When Are Siblings Blocked](/en/blog/when-siblings-blocked-inheritance.html). For spouse shares: [wife's share](/en/blog/wife-inheritance-share.html) or [husband's share](/en/blog/husband-inheritance-share.html).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**My father wants to give my brother more — is that allowed?**
Not in inheritance. But gifts during lifetime are permissible if fair.

**Do schools differ on children's shares?**
No. Children's shares are agreed upon. See [Four Schools Differences](/en/blog/inheritance-madhab-differences.html) for other cases.
