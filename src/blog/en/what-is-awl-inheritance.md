## What Is Awl, Simply?

Imagine dividing a cake: half for one, two-thirds for another, one-sixth for a third. That's more than one cake! Awl is exactly this — total shares exceed the estate. The solution: **all shares are reduced proportionally**.

For share types, see [How Islamic Inheritance Works](/en/blog/how-islamic-inheritance-works.html).

## When Does Awl Occur?

Only when **all heirs are fixed-share holders** and their total exceeds one whole. Never happens with a residuary heir.

## Example 1: Awl from 6 to 7

Woman dies leaving: husband + two full sisters.
- Husband: 1/2 = 3/6 | Sisters: 2/3 = 4/6 | Total: **7/6**

Estate $70,000 ÷ 7: Husband: **$30,000** | Each sister: **$20,000**

## Example 2: Awl from 12 to 15

Man dies leaving: wife, two full sisters, two maternal sisters. Base 12:
- Wife: 3 | Sisters: 8 | Maternal: 4 | Total: **15/12**

Estate $150,000: Wife: **$30,000** | Each full sister: **$40,000** | Each maternal: **$20,000**

## Awl vs Radd

| | Awl | Radd |
|--|-----|------|
| **When** | Shares > estate | Shares < estate, no residuary |
| **Effect** | Shares reduced | Shares increased |

For how Radd differs between schools, see [Four Schools Differences](/en/blog/inheritance-madhab-differences.html).
