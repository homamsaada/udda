## The Core Principle

Giving Zakat to relatives is neither entirely prohibited nor entirely permitted. The rule is: **you cannot give Zakat to someone you are obligated to financially support.** If Islamic law requires you to provide for a person, paying them Zakat would effectively replace your personal obligation with Zakat funds.

## Relatives You CANNOT Give Zakat To

### Parents and Grandparents (Ascendants)

You cannot give Zakat to your father, mother, grandfather, or grandmother. Their financial support is your responsibility when they are in need.

### Children and Grandchildren (Descendants)

You cannot give Zakat to your son, daughter, or grandchildren. For the same reason — their support is your obligation.

### Your Wife (From the Husband)

A husband cannot give his Zakat to his wife because her financial support is his duty. However, a wife may give her Zakat to her husband if he is poor, since she is not obligated to support him.

## Relatives You CAN Give Zakat To

### Brothers and Sisters

You may give Zakat to your siblings if they qualify (poor, needy, or in debt). In fact, this is preferable to giving to strangers because it combines charity with maintaining family ties. The Prophet ﷺ said: "Charity to the poor is charity, and charity to a relative is two things: charity and maintaining ties of kinship."

### Uncles, Aunts (Paternal and Maternal)

You may give them Zakat if they are eligible, as you are not obligated to support them financially.

### Nieces and Nephews

Permissible as well — they are neither your ascendants nor descendants.

### In-Laws

Your father-in-law, mother-in-law, or your spouse's relatives — Zakat may be given to them if they qualify.

## Conditions That Must Be Met

For Zakat to a permitted relative to be valid:

- **They must be eligible**: They must genuinely be poor, needy, in debt, or belong to one of the eight Zakat-eligible categories.
- **You must not be obligated to support them**: As explained above.
- **No hidden benefit**: For example, giving your brother Zakat so he repays a debt he owes you is not valid.
- **Intention**: You must intend the payment as Zakat at the time of giving.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**My father is retired and struggling — can't I give him Zakat?**
No, because his support is your personal obligation. Spend on him from your own wealth, then give Zakat to other eligible recipients.

**My sister is a widow with children — can I give her Zakat?**
Yes, and this is one of the best uses of Zakat as it combines the obligation with strengthening family ties.

**Do I have to tell my relative that the money is from Zakat?**
No, you may give it without mentioning it is Zakat to preserve their dignity.
