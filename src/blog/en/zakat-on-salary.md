## Is Zakat Due on Salary?

Your salary at the moment you receive it is **not subject to Zakat**. This is because Zakat requires the passing of one full lunar year (Hawl) while the money is in your possession. A newly received salary has not completed a Hawl.

**However**, whatever you save from your salary and accumulate in your bank account — once it reaches the Nisab and a full year passes — becomes subject to Zakat.

The practical challenge is that salary comes in monthly, some is spent, and some remains. How do you track which portion has completed a full year?

## Two Methods for Calculating Salary Zakat

### Method 1: Track Each Month Separately (Most Precise)

Theoretically, each monthly salary has its own Hawl. January's salary completes its year in January next year, February's in February, and so on. While precise, this method is exhausting and impractical for most people.

### Method 2: Set a Fixed Annual Zakat Date (Easiest — Recommended)

Choose one day per year (e.g., the 1st of Ramadan) and on that day:

1. Add up everything you own — bank balances, cash, savings, and money owed to you.
2. Subtract currently due debts.
3. If the total reaches the Nisab, pay 2.5%.

Many contemporary scholars approve this method because you may be paying Zakat on some money before its Hawl is complete (advance payment), which is permissible in Islamic law. The benefit is that you pay once a year and avoid complex tracking.

## Practical Example

Ahmad earns $3,000 per month. He spends $2,100 and saves $900 each month.

On the 1st of Ramadan, he checks his finances:

- Bank balance: $12,000
- Cash at home: $800
- Loaned to a colleague: $1,500

Total: $14,300

He has a car payment due: $700

Zakatable amount: $14,300 - $700 = **$13,600**

Does it reach the Nisab? Yes.

**Zakat**: $13,600 × 2.5% = **$340**

He pays once and waits until next Ramadan.

## Practical Tips for Salary Zakat

- **Fix an annual date**: Choose a date you'll remember (1st of Ramadan, start of the Hijri year, or your birthday) and stick with it every year.
- **Don't overcomplicate it**: You don't need to track each paycheck separately. Method 2 is sufficient and accepted by scholars.
- **Money you've spent doesn't count**: What you spent on necessities throughout the year is gone and not included.
- **Don't forget bonuses**: If you received a bonus or incentive and saved it, add it to your Zakatable total.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**My salary barely covers my expenses — do I owe Zakat?**
No. If you save nothing and your balance doesn't reach the Nisab, no Zakat is due.

**Is Zakat due on end-of-service benefits?**
If you receive the payout and it reaches the Nisab and a year passes (or it coincides with your fixed Zakat date), yes.

**Can I pay Zakat monthly instead of annually?**
Yes, as an advance payment. Some people divide their expected annual Zakat by 12 and pay a portion each month. This is permissible and helpful for recipients who receive consistent support.
