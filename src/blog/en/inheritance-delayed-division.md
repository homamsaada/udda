## The Islamic Ruling

The estate transfers to heirs **immediately upon death**. Every heir owns their share from the moment of death.

**Delaying** is permissible only if all heirs agree. If one requests their share, division must happen immediately.

**Withholding inheritance** is among the major sins.

## Real Risks of Delay

**Loss of rights**: Money spent, property sold without consent.

**Compounding complexity**: If an heir dies before division, it becomes "inheritance within inheritance." See [How Islamic Inheritance Works](/en/blog/how-islamic-inheritance-works.html).

**Family disputes**: Undivided wealth breeds conflicts.

**Spiritual liability**: Denying women their inheritance is explicitly prohibited.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**My older brother refuses to divide — what do I do?**
Try dialogue, mediation, then court. His control doesn't cancel your right.

**Can daughters demand their inheritance?**
Absolutely. It's explicitly ordained by God.

**Both parents died and the estate was never divided — how to calculate?**
Divide the first estate first, then the second. For grandchildren cases, see [Do Grandchildren Inherit?](/en/blog/grandchildren-inheritance.html)
