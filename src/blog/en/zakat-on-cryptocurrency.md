## Is Zakat Due on Cryptocurrency?

Yes, according to most contemporary scholars and Islamic jurisprudence bodies, Zakat is due on cryptocurrency. The reason is that crypto has recognized market value and can be traded and converted to cash. Regardless of the debate on whether crypto trading itself is permissible, those who hold it must pay Zakat if it reaches the Nisab and a year has passed.

## How Is Cryptocurrency Classified in Islamic Law?

Scholars have differed on classification, but the main views are:

- **Trade goods**: The majority contemporary view. Crypto is treated like merchandise bought and sold for profit.
- **Customary currency**: Some scholars consider it a form of money since it functions as a medium of exchange with market value.

In both cases, the practical outcome is the same: **pay 2.5% of the market value**.

## How to Calculate Zakat on Cryptocurrency

The method is straightforward despite price volatility:

**Step 1**: Determine your Zakat date (Hawl completion or your fixed annual date).

**Step 2**: On that day, open your wallet or exchange and record the current market value in your local currency.

**Step 3**: Add your crypto value to all other assets (cash, gold, stocks, etc.).

**Step 4**: Subtract currently due debts.

**Step 5**: If the total reaches the Nisab, pay 2.5%.

### Practical Example

Omar owns:

- 0.5 Bitcoin at $65,000/BTC = $32,500
- 3 Ethereum at $3,200/ETH = $9,600
- Other tokens worth: $4,000
- Bank balance: $5,000

Total: $32,500 + $9,600 + $4,000 + $5,000 = **$51,100**

Current debts due: $800

Zakatable amount: $51,100 - $800 = **$50,300**

**Zakat**: $50,300 × 2.5% = **$1,257.50**

## What About Price Volatility?

This is the most confusing aspect for crypto holders. The rule is: **use the value on your Zakat date.** Don't use the all-time high or the lowest dip — just the price on the day you calculate.

If the price drops sharply after your Zakat date, you paid correctly based on that day's value. If it rises, the higher value will be captured in next year's calculation.

## Special Cases

### Mining

Mined cryptocurrency is treated as new income. It is added to your total assets on your Zakat date and included in your overall calculation.

### Staking Rewards

Rewards earned from staking are treated like investment returns. Add them to your total wealth and include them in your Zakat calculation.

### NFTs

If you hold NFTs with the intention of selling for profit, they are trade assets subject to Zakat at market value. NFTs kept for personal use are not Zakatable.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Do I pay Zakat on tokens I can't sell (frozen or on a non-functional network)?**
If you expect to recover them, the safer approach is to include them. If you consider them a loss with no expectation of recovery, they are treated like uncollectible debt — no Zakat until recovered.

**I trade daily — how do I calculate?**
Choose a fixed annual date. On that day, the value of everything in your wallets + cash in your trading account = your Zakatable amount. You don't need to track every trade.

**Some scholars say crypto is haram — is Zakat still due?**
Even scholars who consider crypto trading impermissible still hold that Zakat is due on holdings. The Zakat obligation is not cancelled by doubts about the source.
