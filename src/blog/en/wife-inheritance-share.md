One of the most frequently asked questions about Islamic inheritance is: how much does a wife inherit from her deceased husband? The answer is clearly stated in the Quran but varies depending on whether children or grandchildren exist. This article explains every scenario with practical examples.

## The Wife's Share: Two Cases Only

The Quran specifies the wife's share clearly:

### Case 1: One-Quarter (1/4)

The wife inherits **one-quarter** of the estate if her deceased husband had no descendants — no sons, daughters, or grandchildren (not from her or from anyone else).

### Case 2: One-Eighth (1/8)

The wife inherits **one-eighth** of the estate if her husband had any descendants — a son, daughter, grandson, or granddaughter (whether from her or from another wife).

**Important note**: Descendants from another wife also count. The wife's share is reduced to 1/8 even if the children are not hers.

## Practical Examples

### Example 1: Wife with Children

A man dies leaving: a wife, two sons, and a daughter.

Estate: $200,000.

- Wife: 1/8 = $200,000 ÷ 8 = **$25,000**
- Remainder ($175,000): divided among children — males receive double the female's share
- Each son: $70,000
- Daughter: $35,000

### Example 2: Wife with No Children

A man dies leaving: a wife and two full brothers.

Estate: $100,000.

- Wife: 1/4 = $100,000 ÷ 4 = **$25,000**
- Remainder ($75,000): divided equally between brothers = $37,500 each

### Example 3: Wife with Stepchildren

A man dies leaving: his current wife and a son from a previous (divorced) wife.

Estate: $150,000.

- Current wife: **1/8** (the son is a descendant even though from another woman) = $18,750
- Son: remainder = $131,250

## Multiple Wives and Inheritance

If a man dies with more than one wife (two, three, or four), the wife's share (1/4 or 1/8) is divided equally among them.

### Example

A man dies leaving: two wives and a son.

Estate: $240,000.

- Both wives together: 1/8 = $30,000
- Each wife: $30,000 ÷ 2 = **$15,000**
- Son: remainder = $210,000

## Special Cases

**Wife in revocable divorce (Talaq Raj'i)**: If the husband dies while the wife is in her waiting period from a revocable divorce, she still inherits because the marriage bond still exists.

**Wife after irrevocable divorce (Talaq Ba'in)**: She does not inherit, as the marriage has ended. However, if the husband divorced her during his final illness to prevent her from inheriting, some schools rule that she still inherits.

**Non-Muslim wife**: A non-Muslim wife does not inherit from a Muslim husband, and vice versa.

## The Wife's Other Financial Rights in the Estate

Besides her inheritance share, the wife may have other financial rights that take priority before distribution. If she has a deferred dowry (Mahr Mu'ajjal), it is a debt on the estate that must be paid before division — meaning she receives it first, then takes her share of the remainder. Similarly, any personal funds of hers that were mixed with her husband's assets are separated first. Many people confuse the wife's money with the husband's — for example, if she works and her salary goes into a joint account, her portion of that account is not part of the estate at all.

## Can a Wife Demand Her Share?

Absolutely. The wife's share is a divinely ordained right. No heir may deprive her of it, reduce it, or pressure her to waive it. Doing so is considered unjust appropriation of her property.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does the wife inherit from the marital home?**
Yes. The home is part of the estate. The wife receives her share either as a cash equivalent of the home's value or as co-ownership.

**My husband passed away and only left the house — what do I do?**
The house is part of the estate. You receive your legal share (1/8 or 1/4), and heirs can agree to let you stay while preserving everyone's rights.

**Can my husband's family prevent me from inheriting?**
Absolutely not. This is a religious right that cannot be denied through coercion or custom.
