## What Are the Conditions for Zakat?

Before calculating, make sure these conditions are met:

* **Islam:** Zakat is obligatory only for Muslims.
* **Full ownership:** You must have complete ownership of the wealth and the freedom to use it.
* **Reaching the Nisab:** Your wealth must meet a minimum threshold (the Nisab), explained below.
* **Passing of one lunar year (Hawl):** A full Hijri year must pass while the wealth remains at or above the Nisab.
* **Excess over basic needs:** Zakat applies to wealth beyond your essential needs such as housing, food, and clothing.

## What Is the Nisab?

The Nisab is the minimum amount of wealth that makes Zakat obligatory. If your wealth is below the Nisab, you owe no Zakat.

There are two standards:

* **Gold Nisab:** 85 grams of pure gold (24 karat).
* **Silver Nisab:** 595 grams of silver.

For cash and currencies, the Nisab is calculated based on the equivalent value of either standard. Most contemporary scholars recommend using the silver Nisab as it is lower and ensures more people fulfill this obligation.

**Example:** If the current price of 24k gold is $65 per gram, the gold Nisab = 85 × $65 = **$5,525**. Anyone who owns this amount or more and a full year has passed must pay Zakat.

## What Is the Zakat Rate?

The Zakat rate on wealth is **2.5%**, also known as one-fortieth of your total Zakatable assets. This rate applies to cash, gold, silver, and trade goods.

The formula is simple:

**Zakat = Total Wealth × 2.5%**

Or alternatively: divide your wealth by 40 — the result is your Zakat.

## How to Calculate Zakat on Cash

This is the most common type of Zakat. Follow these steps:

**Step 1:** Add up all your cash — bank accounts, cash on hand, savings deposits, and money you have lent to others (if you expect it to be returned).

**Step 2:** Subtract debts that are currently due (this month's loan installments, outstanding bills). Do not subtract the full amount of long-term debts.

**Step 3:** Compare the result to the Nisab. If it's below, no Zakat is due.

**Step 4:** Multiply the result by 2.5%.

### Practical Example

Ahmad has:

* Bank account: $10,000
* Cash at home: $1,500
* Loaned to a friend: $2,500 (expects repayment)

**Total:** $14,000

He has a car installment due this month: $500

**Zakatable amount:** $14,000 - $500 = **$13,500**

Does it reach the Nisab? Yes (assuming Nisab is $5,525).

**Zakat due:** $13,500 × 2.5% = **$337.50**

## How to Calculate Zakat on Gold

If you own gold (jewelry or bullion), Zakat is due according to the majority of scholars when it reaches **85 grams** of pure gold (24 karat). For 21k or 18k gold, you first calculate the pure gold equivalent.

**Karat conversion:**

* 21k gold: Pure weight = Weight × (21 ÷ 24)
* 18k gold: Pure weight = Weight × (18 ÷ 24)

### Practical Example

Fatimah owns 100 grams of 21k gold.

**Pure weight:** 100 × (21 ÷ 24) = **87.5 grams** of pure gold.

Does it reach the Nisab (85 grams)? **Yes.**

**Value:** 87.5 × $65 (price per gram of 24k) = **$5,687.50**

**Zakat:** $5,687.50 × 2.5% = **$142.19**

## How to Calculate Zakat on Stocks

Stocks have two scenarios:

**If you are a long-term investor** (buying stocks to hold and earn dividends): You pay Zakat only on the dividends received, if they reach the Nisab and a year has passed, at 2.5%.

**If you are an active trader** (buying and selling for profit): You pay Zakat on the full market value of your shares on the day the Hawl is completed, at 2.5%. This is because the shares are treated as trade goods.

### Practical Example

Khalid holds shares for trading purposes, worth $50,000 at market value on his Zakat date.

**Zakat:** $50,000 × 2.5% = **$1,250**

## Common Mistakes in Zakat Calculation

Many Muslims make errors when calculating Zakat. The most common ones include:

* **Forgetting some assets:** Bank deposits, loaned money, or savings funds are often overlooked. All Zakatable assets must be combined.
* **Deducting all debts:** Only currently due debts should be deducted, not the full balance of long-term loans.
* **Delaying Zakat without reason:** Zakat is due immediately when the Hawl is completed. Delaying it without a valid reason is not permissible.
* **Paying Zakat in kind instead of cash:** The default for wealth Zakat is to pay in cash, not food or clothing, except in special circumstances.
* **Confusing Zakat with Sadaqah:** Zakat is an obligation with fixed rates and designated recipients. Sadaqah is voluntary and open-ended.

## The Eight Categories of Zakat Recipients

The Quran specifies eight groups eligible to receive Zakat: **the poor**, **the needy**, **Zakat administrators**, **those whose hearts are to be reconciled**, **freeing captives**, **debtors**, **in the cause of Allah**, and **the stranded traveler**. Zakat may not be given to anyone outside these categories.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Zakat due on my home?** No. Your primary residence, personal car, and household furniture are not Zakatable. However, if you own property with the intention of selling it for profit, Zakat is due on it as a trade asset.

**Do I pay Zakat on savings earmarked for buying a house?** Yes. As long as the money is in your account in cash form, has reached the Nisab, and a full year has passed, Zakat is due — regardless of what you intend to use it for.

**When does the Hawl (year) begin?** It starts from the day your wealth first reaches the Nisab. If your wealth drops below the Nisab during the year and then recovers, the ruling varies by school of jurisprudence.

**Is Zakat due on my monthly salary?** Zakat is not due on your salary the moment you receive it. However, whatever you save from it that reaches the Nisab and remains for a full year is subject to Zakat.
