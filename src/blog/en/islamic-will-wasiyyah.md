## What Is a Wasiyyah?

A bequest directing that a portion of wealth be given to a specific person or cause after death. Unlike inheritance (shares fixed by God), the will is a personal choice within Islamic limits.

## Order of Estate Execution

1. Funeral costs
2. Debt repayment
3. **Will execution** — up to one-third
4. Inheritance distribution

The will comes after debts and before inheritance. For details, see [How Islamic Inheritance Works](/en/blog/how-islamic-inheritance-works.html).

## Conditions for a Valid Will

### 1. Cannot Exceed One-Third
The Prophet ﷺ said: "One-third, and one-third is a lot." If more is bequeathed, only one-third is executed unless heirs approve.

### 2. Not Permitted for an Heir
The Prophet ﷺ said: "No bequest for an heir." Exception: if remaining heirs approve after death.

### 3. Must Not Be Sinful

## Who Should You Bequeath To?

- **Grandchildren from a deceased child**: If they'll be blocked by your other sons. See [Do Grandchildren Inherit?](/en/blog/grandchildren-inheritance.html)
- **Charities**: Ongoing charity (Sadaqah Jariyah)
- **Non-inheriting relatives** in need
- **Beneficial causes**: Mosque, well, education

## How to Write a Valid Will

- Write clearly or type and sign
- Have two trustworthy witnesses
- Store a copy with a trusted person
- Review and update periodically

## The Obligatory Bequest (In Law)

## Common Mistakes

- Bequeathing all wealth (invalid beyond 1/3)
- Bequeathing to an heir (not permissible)
- Leaving it to the last moment
- No witnesses
- Failing to update

To avoid post-death division problems, see [Delaying Estate Division](/en/blog/inheritance-delayed-division.html).

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is a will obligatory?**
Recommended. Becomes obligatory if you hold trusts only you know about.

**Can I revoke my will?**
Yes, at any time while alive.

**Must it be officially registered?**
Not religiously, but advisable legally.
