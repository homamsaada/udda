## The Husband's Share: Two Cases

### Case 1: One-Half (1/2)
No descendants → husband inherits **half**.

### Case 2: One-Quarter (1/4)
Descendants exist → husband inherits **one-quarter**.

**Note**: The husband's share is higher than the wife's in equivalent cases because he bears financial responsibility for the family.

## Practical Examples

**With children**: Wife dies leaving husband + two sons. Estate: $150,000. Husband: 1/4 = **$37,500**. Sons share the rest.

**No children**: Wife dies leaving husband + full brother. Estate: $100,000. Husband: 1/2 = **$50,000**. Brother: $50,000.

**Stepchildren**: Wife dies leaving current husband + son from previous marriage. Estate: $120,000. Husband: **1/4** = $30,000. Son: $90,000.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Does the husband inherit from his wife's mahr?**
Yes, the mahr is her property and part of her estate.

**My wife worked — do I inherit her personal money?**
Yes. For blocking rules see [When Are Siblings Blocked](/en/blog/when-siblings-blocked-inheritance.html).

**Do schools differ?**
The husband's share is agreed upon. Differences appear in Radd — see [Four Schools Differences](/en/blog/inheritance-madhab-differences.html).
