One of the most common questions Muslim women ask is: do I have to pay Zakat on the gold jewelry I wear? This question has been debated by scholars since the time of the Prophet's companions, and both sides have strong evidence. In this article, we present the different views, the strongest opinion, and how to calculate if Zakat is due.

## What Is "Worn Gold"?

Worn gold refers to gold jewelry kept for personal adornment and regularly used — rings, bracelets, necklaces, and earrings. This does not include gold stored away and never worn, which is subject to Zakat by consensus.

## Scholarly Views on Zakat for Worn Gold

### View 1: Zakat Is Obligatory

This is the Hanafi position, supported by several companions including Abdullah ibn Umar and Abdullah ibn Amr. They cite the general Quranic verses and hadiths requiring Zakat on gold and silver without distinction. This includes the hadith where the Prophet ﷺ saw gold bangles on a woman's daughter and asked if she paid Zakat on them, warning of punishment if she did not.

### View 2: Zakat Is Not Obligatory

This is the majority view (Maliki, Shafi'i, and Hanbali schools), provided the jewelry is for normal personal use and not excessive. They argue that personal jewelry is similar to clothing and shelter — items of personal necessity exempt from Zakat. They also cite reports that Aisha (may Allah be pleased with her) adorned her nieces with gold without paying Zakat on it.

### The Stronger Position

Contemporary scholars differ in their conclusions. Sheikh Ibn Baz and Sheikh Ibn Uthaymeen held that Zakat is obligatory on worn gold as a precaution based on the apparent meaning of the hadiths. Others follow the majority view of non-obligation.

**The safest approach** is to pay Zakat on worn gold to avoid the scholarly disagreement and earn reward. However, following the majority view is also a valid position.

## How to Calculate Zakat on Gold Jewelry

If you decide to pay, follow these steps:

**First**: Determine the weight of your gold in grams. Any jeweler can weigh it for you.

**Second**: Convert to pure gold (24 karat):
- 21k gold: Pure weight = Weight × (21 ÷ 24)
- 18k gold: Pure weight = Weight × (18 ÷ 24)

**Third**: Check if the pure weight reaches the Nisab (85 grams of pure gold).

**Fourth**: Multiply the market value by 2.5%.

### Practical Example

Fatimah owns:
- A 21k bracelet weighing 30 grams
- A 21k necklace weighing 25 grams
- 18k ring and earrings weighing 20 grams

Pure weight calculation:
- 21k gold: (30 + 25) × (21 ÷ 24) = 48.1 grams
- 18k gold: 20 × (18 ÷ 24) = 15 grams
- Total pure gold: 48.1 + 15 = **63.1 grams**

Does it reach the Nisab (85 grams)? No. Therefore, no Zakat is due in this case.

If her total pure gold were 100 grams at $65 per gram (24k):
- Value: 100 × $65 = $6,500
- **Zakat**: $6,500 × 2.5% = **$162.50**

## Cases Where Zakat Is Due by Consensus

Some types of gold are subject to Zakat regardless of scholarly opinion:

- **Stored or hoarded gold**: Gold you keep but never wear.
- **Gold held for sale**: Gold purchased as an investment or for trading.
- **Excessive amounts**: Quantities far beyond normal personal use.
- **Gold bars and bullion**: Even if kept at home.

## Frequently Asked Questions

**Is Zakat due on white gold?**
White gold is real gold alloyed with other metals to change its color. It is treated the same as regular gold for Zakat purposes.

**Is Zakat due on diamonds and gemstones?**
No Zakat is due on diamonds and gemstones for personal use. Zakat applies only if they are held for trade.

**My husband gave me the gold — who pays the Zakat?**
If the gold is yours (gift or mahr), the Zakat is your responsibility. However, your husband may pay it on your behalf with your permission.
